# Economics

## Lead value

The goal of this product is not direct revenue from the audit itself. The audit is a free tool that helps users understand where they may be overspending on AI subscriptions.

For Credex, the value comes from identifying users or teams already spending money on AI tools and turning some of them into customers for AI credits or future products.

A realistic qualified lead for Credex would be:

- a startup founder already paying for multiple AI tools
- a small engineering team using paid plans
- someone exploring AI costs before scaling

Even if only a small number convert, these leads are much more relevant than generic traffic because they already have spending intent.

## Customer acquisition cost

My first users would likely come from:

- friends in college building side projects
- hackathon teams
- startup/student communities
- posting in IIT Kanpur groups or builder communities

So initial acquisition cost is effectively very low.

Main cost is time spent sharing the product and collecting feedback.

## Funnel

A realistic first funnel could look like this:

100 people visit the landing page

→ around 40 start the audit

→ around 25 complete it

→ around 8 share the report or discuss results

→ around 2–3 are genuinely interested in reducing spend

→ a smaller subset may become leads for Credex

The important part is that the users reaching the final step are highly relevant because they already use paid AI products.

## Why this matters

The audit acts like a lightweight entry point.

Instead of asking users to sign up immediately, it gives value first:

- shows pricing
- compares plans
- estimates savings

That makes lead capture feel more natural.

For Credex, this can help surface:

- startups scaling AI usage
- teams exploring multiple vendors
- users actively thinking about spend

which are likely strong long-term customers.
